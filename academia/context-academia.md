# Personal Context

## Background

In 2022 I Enrolled into Bachelor of Software engineering at Monash University.
This is generally a 4 year course. The first year is general engineering while the last 3 are specialised to the specialisation you pick.
It also calls for about 70 hours of industry experience (internships, student teams etc).

Unfortunately between 2022 and 2024 I failed a significant number of units. I attributes this to poor habits, and a failure to cope with my new schedule of work + gym + social obligations + uni. I was thrust into an adult world without the skill necessary to operate. This was a very tough time for me and I live with the consequences of this to this day. I say this from a literal sense (2 year delay on my course) but also in a psychological sense. This short coming weight heavy on me psycologically too. Going from a high achieving background to this was tough at the time.

However following this phase I built better habbits, surrounded myself with the right people and turned things around to where I do all the thing I need (work, school, gym, social etc) while maintaining quite decent academic standards. However the failing of the past still weighheavy and I feel a sense of deep shame and regret about my past shortcomings.

This has affected everything as it has delayed my graduation. This means it has delayed my entry into the industry (and thus my financial and life goals too). I find that I attribute every shortcoming in large part to this (such as a lack of a relationship, a financial situation Im not contempt with etc).

Ultimately I am committed to this meteoric rise and radical growth mindset and I now persue my goals like a killer; however jaded I am from past failings.
